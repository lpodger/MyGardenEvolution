Gardening Tips

  • Observe your climate and local conditions, some plants will grow better or swiftly die depending on how closely your conditions mimic their natural environment.
  • The right plant for the right spot, don't force things to grow where they are ill suited, find something that likes those conditions and work with it.
  • Pay attention to where the sun and shade are in your garden and how that moves depending on the time of day and year.
  • If you're growing vegetables or anything for production, then make sure they recieve over 6 hours of direct sunlight to get the most out of your harvest.
  • Cultivate a healthy, living soil, that retains moisture and everything will fall into place.
  • Ensure adequate irrigation, push your index finger into the soil to see if infiltration has occured or if your soil is hydrophobic.
  • Mulch is both aesthetic and functional, helping to retain moisture and build up fertility as it breaks down over time.
Soil Types and Their Qualities
Soil Type Water Retention Watering Frequency
Sandy Low High
Loamy Medium Medium
Clay High Low

From the table above you can see that generally the best soil to work towards is loamy soil. It holds moisture and nutrients the best and provides the best conditions for most plants. You can build your soil quality over time by adding organic matter which breaks down over time and promotes beneficial organisms and micro-organisms to colonise the area. This is what we refer to as a "living soil".
